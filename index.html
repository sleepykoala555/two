1. Introduction

In this project, I will attempt to identify and analyze the various usability problems of the Craigslist website, then redesign the website with prototyping and lastly build a website in HTML/CSS that’s responsive to three different screen frames (desktop, tablet, and phone).


2. Website - Craigslist

Website Link: https://sfbay.craigslist.org/

Introduction: Craigslist (stylized as craigslist) is an American classified advertisements website with sections devoted to jobs, housing, for sale, etc.

Reasons: I’m intending to relocate to San Francisco after my graduation, so I’ve been looking for housing on various websites and applications. The design of Craigslist completely stunned me because it’s so rudimentary despite being one of the most visited websites in the world. When I first used it, I was overwhelmed by the packed information and confused about how to navigate the website effectively to find the house I want.

3. Craigslist Usability Problems

  1. Overwhelming information - too much information is given on the same page so it’s hard to navigate and find relevant information.
  2. Lack of hierarchy but lots of distractions - all items have the same weight visually, yet the main lists should be given more emphasis while the lists on the side are very distractive.
  3. Lack of visual clues such as icons - there is no icon or other design that gives a clear visual clue on where to look at. It can be hard to use for people who are not good at reading texts.
  4. Not responsive - the website is not responsive to changes in window sizes on desktops and tablets, so I have to scroll left and right to browse the website.
  5. Aesthetics - Visually, it is horrible to look at the page as a whole, especially for someone with Trypophobia.
  6. No preliminary filer - since there’s no preliminary filter for each sub-category, after I click, for example, “apts/housing”, pictures of many houses will appear while only a few are relevant.
  7. Learnability - The filter and search are difficult to use, which makes it difficult for users to find the items they looking for.
  8. Memorability - it’s hard to remember all the categories and subcategories, so every time I open it and use it. I have to look through the lists again until I find the right one.

4. Accessibility Analysis

Wave Report: I use the WebAIM WAVE tool to detect possible accessibility problems. Here's what I agree and disagree with findings from the WAVE report.

Agree Disagree
  • Alerts for texts too small - I agree because the texts, especially under the header “discussion forums” are very small and difficult to read.
  • Missing from label - I agree since many selected items don’t have labeling so it’s hard to know how to interact with the items.
  • Skipping from heading level - I agree because both “calendar” and “nearby cl” should be headers but instead, their fonts are not larger, so the hierarchy in texts is not clear enough.
  • No errors for contrast - I was surprised because although there is no contrast between the white backgrounds and the blue text, all the texts are in very the same color so there is no contract between texts that highlight their relationships, importance, and hierarchy. Also, the text turns from blue to dark purple once clicked so there’s no much contrast.
  • No errors for features - there is a lack of variety in features but only hypertexts.

Conclusion: the accessibility website only tests craigslist according to certain rigid principles but since it cannot process the texts and understand the hierarchy and meanings of information presented on the website, accessibility can capture errors in design principles but not some higher-level accessibility issues.
